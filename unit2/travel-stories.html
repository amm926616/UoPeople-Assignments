Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower in Paris, France. It is one of the most iconic landmarks in the world.

Designed by Gustave Eiffel and completed in 1889, it was initially criticized but is now a global symbol of France.

The tower stands at 330 meters (1,083 feet) tall and attracts millions of visitors annually.

It has three observation decks offering panoramic views of Paris, and it lights up beautifully at night with a dazzling display.

Colosseum

The Colosseum is an ancient amphitheater in Rome, Italy. It was used for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles.

Constructed between 72-80 AD under Emperor Vespasian and his successor Titus, it is the largest ancient amphitheater ever built.

It could hold up to 50,000 spectators and hosted events such as gladiator battles, animal hunts, and mock sea battles.

Despite earthquakes and stone plundering, it remains a significant historical site and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty is a colossal neoclassical sculpture on Liberty Island in New York Harbor. It symbolizes freedom and democracy.

A gift from France to the United States in 1886, it was designed by Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and built by Gustave Eiffel.

The statue is 93 meters (305 feet) tall, including its pedestal, and features a torch and a tablet inscribed with July 4, 1776.

It is recognized as a universal symbol of hope and welcomes immigrants arriving by sea.
